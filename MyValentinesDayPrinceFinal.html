February 14th, 2*** One Year Ago

Nanami Fujiwara didn’t know when it started.

It wasn’t like she asked to be the most popular girl in school. And yet she was.

There was certainly precedent for it, of course. She was insanely smart, and possessed an athletic strength that no one could possibly hope to tangle with.

It was surely hard to believe that a ten year old girl held such traits.

Coupled alongside her amicable, yet firm, personality, Nanami was the kind of person everyone wanted to be friends with.

But that was to her absolute detriment.

See, for as many admirers and supposed friends she had, a good majority of them were only near her because of the connections. How great it would be, to say you were friends with the girl who was considered the queen of her elementary school!

Nanami probably only had two close friends who were by her side for personal reasons, rather than artificial ones.

Worst of all, it seemed Nanami’s school locker was a mystery box of letters, gifts, and adoration. She had so many confessions, it even got to the point where she stopped seeing any of them as genuine.

That led to her being called “The Cold Princess”. An urban legend spread stating that no boy could ever sway her hand.

Nanami knew the rumors, and she hated them. It wasn’t like she was against the idea of dating. She just didn’t want someone who would only use her as an object for bragging.

Sure, she carried herself like a haughty popular girl, but she was simply an innocent maiden deep down.

Unfortunately, in the eyes of many, including herself, it was unlikely she would ever find the one.

That is, until the Little Prince arrived.

He seemed innocuous at first, almost ridiculous even. His blue and white costume seemed bright and jarring compared to all of the sickening pink and red Nanami had to endure at school several hours earlier.

But there was something about his aura that stood out to her. In the midst of all of the usual hubbub of school and popularity, this seemed rather fresh.

It wasn’t like he called out to her either. She was just walking home after a busy day and was drawn to him, as he sat alone in a patch of grass.

“Um, hello there.” She greeted, rather cautiously but also kind of curiously.

“Good day to you, fair maiden. I see you’ve been drawn to me, just as I hoped.” The prince replied, turning to her with a smile.

Nanami caught a glance at his eyes. Emerald green, a striking color, to say the least.

“Drawn to you? I just happened to notice you, that’s all.” She said, still a bit apprehensive.

“Ah, yes, but the fact that you’ve noticed me brings happiness to my heart, for I’ve been waiting for you for a long time.” The prince replied, a kind smile on his face.

“You act as if you know me.” Nanami said, narrowing her eyes.

“Oh, that’s because I do, my lady. I know a lot of things. But though I do know you, I regret to say that I know very little about you personally. Why don’t you take a seat, and we talk?” Suggested the prince.

Nanami hesitated, but not feeling immediate danger, she decided to sit down next to him.

“Alright. Let’s talk then. First things first, are you a real prince?” She asked carefully.

The little prince chuckled.

“In a manner of speaking. I would actually say that I’m a humble boy in search of a fair lady.”

Nanami eyed him curiously. She was interested in learning more about him.

“A fair lady? Aren’t all boys that way?” She asked, recalling bad memories.

“Alas, it is true. I’ve seen the worst of it. But I’ve traveled across distant lands and battled many a beast to see this lady of mine.” Said the prince proudly.

His words couldn’t possibly be true. Nanami knew that for certain. And yet…

“Do you often travel to far off places? Do you have many grand adventures, Mr Prince?”

The prince nodded, grinning.

“Of course. That is the way of the prince. But my most recent journey has brought me here, to this wondrous place, for a very special reason.”

“Tell me more!” Nanami said eagerly.

“Well, it’s quite simple. You see, I’ve come seeking the aforementioned lady of ten years old, named Nanami. She’s a delightful person, that I know.”

Nanami blushed, looking away from the prince to hide the red on her face.

“M-me? B-but I’m just a normal girl. What do I have to offer a prince?”

“Let’s find out, shall we? What is your favorite dessert?”

“Um, lemon cake.”

“A good choice. What’s your favorite type of flower?”

“Flowers…probably violets. I love violet roses.”

“Sounds lovely. What is your favorite subject?”

“Math.”

“Do you have any siblings?”

“I’m an only child.”

“Okay, lastly…what is your favorite pastime?”

“…promise you won’t tell anyone?”

“Of course. I’ll keep all of your secrets, my lady.”

“Mm…fine! I really like going to arcades!”

The little prince smiled. He seemed to glow in a sort of way that nearly blinded Nanami, but also seemed to draw her in.

“Well, judging from all that you’ve told me, there are plenty of great things you have to offer, and I accept all of them.”

Nanami couldn’t quite put her finger on it, but his positivity was doing something to her. He was really genuine about all of this, and she felt sort of conflicted.

“You’re very kind, Mr Prince.”

The little prince’s laughter twinkled like wind chimes on the calm breeze, sending unknown sensations through her brain.

“I’m honored to receive your praise. Now I must give you something as well.” He said, pulling out an envelope and box of chocolates.

“I hope you receive these as a sign of my travels for you.”

Nanami took the gifts, but hesitated to accept them as her own. She was accustomed to receiving Valentine’s Day affection, but why did this feel different?

“I can’t accept this, Mr Prince. We literally just met. I don’t know a whole lot about you.” She said, sighing.

The little prince nodded in apparent understanding, standing rather suddenly. He held out his hand with a smile.

“I see. In that case, why don’t we accompany each other for the rest of this beautiful day? Maybe you’ll come to know more about me?”

Nanami couldn’t quite remember what happened next. All she knew was she took the prince’s hand, and after that, time passed in a blur.

She recalled bits and pieces, like how they visited an arcade and won lots of tickets, or how they tried crepes at a new dessert shop, but the day was so fun, she didn’t really care what exactly they did. She just liked that they were together the whole time.

There were certain quirks to the prince, like how he already knew about some techniques in arcade games, or how he referenced school things Nanami never told him about, but she looked past all of that.

However, the day had to come to an end eventually. As the sun set in the distance, Nanami realized with a pang of sadness that they would have to separate.

“It’s getting dark.” She muttered in disappointment.

“Yes, it appears so. I’m afraid our time has drawn to a close.”

Nanami frowned, trying not to show the sadness in her eyes.

“Will I see you again?” She asked, her voice barely above a whisper.

The little prince smiled, placing his hand in hers.

“Fear not, fair maiden, for our paths shall cross in the future. On this same day next year, we shall meet once more. By that time, I trust you will have thought of me.”

He intertwined their fingers, which caused Nanami to blush again.

“Cherish this day. For when we meet next, I will ask you if you remember it.”

Nanami hesitated to let go of his hand, but she knew there was no point in doing so. Reluctantly, she parted ways with him, looking at his receding form with a sense of longing.

“The same day…next year…you better remember, Mr Prince.” She muttered.

And so the days passed as Nanami continued to think of the prince who moved her.

He had been the one to change things, and yet he disappeared as quickly as he appeared.

Nanami often wondered: who was he? Where did he actually come from?

Most importantly, would they actually see each other again?

Only a year’s worth of time would tell.

February 14th, 2*** One Year Later

Naturally, one year isn’t enough to change a person’s public perception.

Nanami was still queen of her elementary school and had a highly desirable aura.

She still excelled in her classes and had athletic superiority.

But lately she seemed…preoccupied. A lot more stringent than usual.

The most recent confessions ended a lot more aggressively compared to the past.

What happened over the course of a year?

Well, we already know. Now that Nanami had met her prince, she was even less impressed with the kids in her classes. None of them could compare.

Is that an unfair comparison? Maybe.

Her friends, Reiko and Aoi, were always quick to tell her as such.

“Nanami, you can’t keep hesitating like this!”

“Yeah, you got to cave in eventually.”

Nanami chuckled.

“I’m good, thanks.”

Nanami opened her locker, and tons of valentines poured out. She winced and groaned.

“I hate this.”

“Why? You should be thankful for all this attention.”

“Don’t need it.”

It was true. She never felt the need for such things anymore.

What was the point of dating someone who didn’t actually do anything for her? Was she supposed to fall for every person who sent her a Valentine’s Day card?

“So much wasted paper for such a wasted day.”

Nanami muttered, to the both amusement and annoyance of her friends.

Reiko and Aoi couldn’t quite understand her. Sure, they loved her to death, and would do anything for her in a heartbeat, but she also had some quirks they couldn’t wrap their heads around.

Despite there being a few boys worth giving interest to, Nanami seemed to always turn everyone down.

Often her words were accompanied by whispers of being incomparable and princes.

Nanami seemed to love talking about princes. But why?

One time they decided to ask her about it…

“Nanami, what’s all this talk about a prince?”

“A prince? Oh, you must mean the little prince. We promised to meet again…he was so great…no one can compare to his kindness…”

Naturally, her answer made no sense to them, but to Nanami, it seemed the most obvious thing in the world.

Will I see you again, Mr Prince?

Nanami sighed and glanced out the window, taking little heed to the monotonous words of the current lesson she was in.

The light of the sun seemed to glow around her, giving her form an ethereal effect. Nearby students couldn’t help but stare at her, entranced.

Naturally, she noticed each pair of locked eyes, and felt extremely tempted to punch every single one.

Fortunately, Nanami couldn’t be bothered. Her thoughts were elsewhere. The colors blue and white seemed to flutter through her mind all the time now.

You promised…

And her heart remained eternally wrapped by a promise.

She wanted him back.

The prince who twisted her so.

Today, some hour, some second, he would appear. But that didn’t feel soon enough.

In an ironic twist of fate, the end of class arrived just as she reached the conclusion of her thoughts.

“Ah…it’s over…” Nanami groaned.

She tiredly gathered her things and lept for the door eagerly.

What a solitary existence.

To have so many admirers following her with their eyes, and yet only two friends.

Nanami frowned and sighed as she walked through the busy hallways. At that moment, something in a secluded classroom caught her eye.

A group of students were standing around a shaking boy, yelling at him with grins on their faces.

“You loser! You’ll never be cool like us! As long as we stand over you, you’ll do what we say!”

“I…I want to get to class…” The scared boy muttered.

“Shut it, skimpy.”

“Ahahaha! That’s a good one!”

They laughed at his fear. Nanami witnessed all of this and could stand to watch it any longer.

“Hey, what are you doing to that boy?”

She cried, startling them as she stormed in.

“Fujiwara-san! We were just giving him what he deserves! You don’t have to worry about this.”

Nanami slapped the bully angrily.

“Don’t say that. Get away from him. Now.”

She snapped.

The bullies scrambled out the room, leaving only her and the shaking boy.

“They must have really roughed you up.”

She muttered.

Nanami looked down and really took in the boy’s appearance for the first time since entering the room.

She knew him.

He was Takahashi Sora, a classmate who sat a few seats in front of her in class.

Other students seemed to always be whispering about him, and Nanami often felt bad for the boy.

Sure, he looked somewhat frail, and his black hair often covered his eyes, but he didn’t deserve the horrible words he received.

“Hey, are you—”

But before she could say anything to him, he ran off. Nanami could only look at his receding form worriedly.

“I hope he’s alright…” She whispered.

“Nanami.”

“Yes?”

“It’s already lunch, and nothing has changed.”

Once again, Nanami received a critical rant from her friends, Reiko and Aoi, about dating.

“We’re really doing this again?” She asked in annoyance.

“This is for your own good, Nana.” Reiko snapped.

“Yeah, and we don’t want to hear about princes this time.” Aoi added.

Nanami sighed in frustration.

“But I really saw him. We promised.”

“Promises can be broken!” Reiko shouted.

“You’re so passionate about all this.” Nanami frowned.

“Of course. It’s not like that prince of yours will just appear—”

The sound of footsteps abruptly paused their conversation.

“My fair ladies.”

Nanami’s eyes went wide in recognition. She stood up suddenly, tears threatening to fall.

“Nana? Is that…?”

It was. The blue and white dressy uniform. The blonde hair. The smile.

“My fair Nanami. I do apologize for interrupting your private time, but I wished to see you after so long away.” Said the undoubtable voice of the little prince.

Nanami stuttered hard.

“M-m-Mr Prince?”

“Yes, I am here.”

The little prince got down on one knee and kissed Nanami on her hand. Her face turned a bright shade of red, and her friends followed suit.

“I wanted to give you some gifts on this great day, Nanami. I hope you accept them.”

The little prince pulls out a bouquet of beautiful violet roses and a purple envelope. He hands both of them to her, and she shakily accepts them.

“You…you remembered…”

“Of course. I will never forget anything you’ve told me.”

For the first time in her life, Nanami felt publicly embarrassed. She wanted to hide herself, for she knew she was blushing extremely hard. At the same time, she really wanted to tell the prince how she felt about him, but…she just couldn’t get over her nervousness.

“T-thank you for these, Mr. Prince. But how did you know I was here? Are you a student at this school? What’s your name?” She asked.

“Student? I’m a prince, my lady.”

“No, you’re…you have to be someone here at school…who are you? I’d really like to know.”

The little prince looked to the ground.

“I can’t tell you.” He nervously whispered, going somewhat out of character.

Suddenly, he ran away before Nanami could say anything else to him. She felt guilty for pressing him on such a subject but she just really wanted to know…

“Nanami, you have great taste.”

“I’m impressed, Nana.”

Nanami eyed her friends in disappointment.

“So now you’re fine with it?”

Back to classes, Nanami found herself surprisingly paired up with the black haired boy from earlier, Sora. They were assigned math work together.

“H-hello…”

He shyly greeted her.  

“Hi, Takahashi-kun. Let’s get this done with, shall we? I’m good at math, so this’ll go quickly!” Nanami said confidently.

“You don’t have to tell me…” Sora muttered.

They found themselves getting through their work fairly easily.

Nanami was honestly surprised by how well they got along. Sora was a little shy, but he spoke up often enough to where conversations continued naturally.

In the end, she felt that there was something familiar about him. His presence gave her a comfort which she could recall…

“Fujiwara-san?”

Sora looked up from the paper he had been writing on, feeling her stares.

Amongst his black hair, Nanami noticed a color. Green. Like emeralds.

Why did that seem important to her? Did she see that color before? There was something she knew, but she couldn’t remember…

“It’s nothing. I was just thinking about something…” She said, assuring.

Sora eyed her curiously and then returned to looking at his paper. Then he muttered:

“Thanks for earlier…”

Nanami’s eyes went wide, and then she smiled.

“Of course.”

Nanami left school feeling tired. Not that that was a rare occurrence.

But today it seemed particularly worse than usual.

“Hey Nana, look there!”

Reiko whispered, pointing near the school gate. Nanami followed her gaze and a large smile grew on her face.

“Mr Prince!” She cried, to the surprise of those near her (including her friends). They had never heard her use such an emotional voice.

Nanami ran over to the gate, where a figure in a blue and white attire stood. Her prince had come once again.

“My lady, you look tired. Allow me to soothe your wounds.” The little prince said, holding out his hand.

Nanami took him on his offer almost immediately.

“Yes!” She exclaimed.

Her surprised classmates watched as the notorious “cold princess” willingly walked off with a boy, hand in hand.

“What shall we do today, Mr Prince?” Nanami asked eagerly.

“Anything you desire.” The little prince responded.

The following hours were better than Nanami could have ever imagined. They went to a restaurant, shared desserts, and ended with an arcade. Memories of the day one year ago resurfaced.

“This day has been so fun…” Nanami muttered as she played an arcade game.

“I agree. Your company always brings me joy, my lady.” The little prince agreed.

Nanami laughed.

“If only the boys in my class were half as nice as you are.”

As if on cue, loud voices began to reach their ears.

“Oi, Fujiwara-san!”

It was the group of bullies from earlier.

“Stand back, my lady.” Said the little prince, putting distance between them.

“Out of the way, idiot. We aren’t here for you.”

One of the bullies sneered.

“I won’t let you harm this lady.” The little prince said firmly, though he was beginning to shake.

“We won’t hurt her. We just want to talk…”

“As if! I have no business with you bullies!” Nanami yelled.

“Shut up!”

One of the bullies cried, throwing powerful punches towards her. Unfortunately, the little prince took every punch himself, getting bruised and injured. Nanami couldn’t stand to watch it.

“How dare you?!” She screamed, charging at the bullies wildly in anger. She punched and kicked them without caring who she hit, and as the dust settled, they were all knocked clean out.

“Ow…” The little prince groaned.

Nanami ran over to him worriedly.

“Mr Prince!”

“I’m glad you are safe…my lady…” He smiled, before fainting.

Nanami caught a glance of his eyes just as this happened, and a sudden connection clicked in her mind.

Green. Like emeralds.

How did it take this long for her to realize the significance?

She knew who this prince was.

The little prince woke in the middle of a park. He found himself lying in Nanami’s lap (she was blushing).

“You’re alright!”

Nanami exclaimed, breathing a sigh of relief. She held him close.

At that moment, the little prince realized something. A wig lying nearby. It couldn’t be…

“Did you see—?”

Nanami nodded.

“You…you’re Takahashi-kun, aren’t you?” She asked.

The prince, now identified as Sora, sighed.

“You weren’t supposed to find out…”

“Why would you hide this from me? Couldn’t you have just told me all this time?” Nanami asked, slightly upset.

“Well, I often feel…scared. Like I’m the only one in this world who’s weird, while everyone else is normal. I get terrified that my actions are always going to be judged, even when it’s something simple. I don’t really like talking to people if I don’t know them. I just…feel like a huge failure…like I could break at any second…”

Nanami hugged him and held him close.

“So, the prince…?”

Sora looked to the ground.

“For some reason, I feel more confident when I’m dressed that way. Honestly, I…I was scared that whole day we spent together. It felt like a long dream that I would have to wake up from, and—”

Nanami rubbed his hair lovingly.

“Sora, if this is a dream, I never want to wake up from it. This day, and the previous we spent, were more fun than anything I’ve ever done. The only ones judging you are those who don’t understand how wonderful you are. If it makes you feel any better, I’ve never once judged you for anything. You’re very kind, Sora.”

Those words. He remembered hearing them before on this day a year ago, and at that time they made him so happy. And here she was one year later giving him everything he could possibly want.

Those words…

Sora chuckled softly. Nanami’s eyes went wide in reminiscence.

“I’m honored to receive your praise…” he whispered, smiling.

The brightest shade of red formed on Nanami’s face as she stuttered.

“Y-you remember everything?”

“Of course. I never forgot a single word, Nanami. I feel comfortable when I talk to you. You’re the only one who makes me feel this way.”

“I…I never stopped thinking about that day…and yet you were near me this whole time…I feel so awful for not helping you sooner…all those nasty words I’ve heard…”

Sora shook his head vigorously.

“You don’t have to feel bad…it was my problem anyway. Besides, I…I don’t want to see you get hurt on my behalf.”

Nanami frowned.

“You really are such a kind person. Why do people judge you? It’s just like they do with me, isn’t it? They assume so many things without getting to know me, and it’s the same with you. I hate it.”

Sora frowned.

“I guess we all have hidden things, huh? We both carry secret sides…but I’m glad to know yours.”

Nanami chuckled weakly.

“Are you trying to flirt right now? While we’re having this emotional moment?”

“It wasn’t intentional. I guess I’m more bold in this outfit…”

“Pfft!” Nanami laughed.

Sora smiled. “Thank you, Nanami. For being by my side. I had always seen you from afar, and…I liked you, but I never thought I could be with you…”

Nanami shook her head, blushing.

“No, I should be the one saying that. Thank you, Sora.”

She looked to the ground.

“It's just…you didn’t have to do all of this for me to like you…”

Sora shrugged.

“I…I didn’t know what else to do…” He muttered.

Nanami giggled.

“Say, how old are you, Sora?” She asked.

“E-eleven…” Sora responded.

“Wait. You’re older than me?!” Nanami exclaimed.

Before Sora could say anything in response to this, it slowly began to rain around them. They looked frantically for shelter.

“Sora, come home with me!” Nanami said firmly.

“What?”

At Nanami’s home, they dried off in silence.

Sitting together on the couch, Nanami was blushing intensely. She was extremely tempted to grab Sora’s hand and confide in him. But she wasn’t bold enough for that.

“It’s pretty rough out there.” She noted nervously.

“I’ll…I’ll stay here until it stops…” Sora said.

“Sure, let’s go do something to pass the—”

CRACK!

The lights went out. It was pitch black in the house.

Sora looked around, but he couldn’t see a single thing. Then he realized Nanami was hugging him.

“Nanami?”

He could feel her shaking and murmuring in fear.

“Please…not now…not now…”

Sora never thought Nanami was capable of such emotion. What was she scared of?

“Nanami, are you alright?”

She shook her head.

“I…I don’t like lightning…I don’t like thunder…it reminds me of…bad times—”

CRACK!

“AAAH!”

Nanami’s shaking grew more violent. Sora felt bad for her. There must have been deeper sides to her than he even knew.

“It’s okay, Nanami. Don’t be scared…” Sora said softly, rubbing her back with soft movements.

Nanami’s breathing slowed and she ceased shaking as much.

“Y-you’re good at this…” She murmured.

“I have siblings…” Sora explained.

For a few minutes they sat there in silence, finding solitude in each other's arms and the sound of rain.

Thunder and lightning didn’t bother them anymore.

Overwhelmed by everything, Nanami couldn’t hold back her words.

“I like you.” She whispered.

Sora’s eyes went wide, as he heard the words he never thought he’d hear.

He couldn’t fathom a way to respond, and so he said nothing. But deep down they knew their feelings were equal.

An hour passed, and the rain finally subsided.

As Sora made for the door, they stared at each other.

“I guess it’s time to leave.” He muttered.

“Yeah.” Nanami agreed.

Yet neither wanted to let each other go.

“We’ll meet again, right?” Nanami asked.

Sora had a blip of recognition and then smiled.

“I’ll see you tomorrow.” He said, getting on one knee and kissing her hand.

Nanami blushed, but said nothing as Sora left her home. She felt all kinds of warm ways and didn’t know what to do.

“I like you so much, Sora…” She muttered, intoxicated by her own love.

At school the next day, Nanami was greeted by glaring eyes. Many people were upset by the prince yesterday.

But Nanami paid them no heed. She was purely happy. Everyone noticed this, and even they couldn’t help but see a positive change.

Her coldness seemed to have been shed, and her demeanor carried the happiness of a regular girl. Nanami had been born anew.

“You look happy.” Reiko said with a grin.

“I am.” Nanami responded, smiling.

Even her friends were surprised by this new Nanami. She was just so different.

But that was nothing compared to what happened later.

This new Nanami walked into her morning class without stopping at her normal seat. People’s eyes curiously followed her, and went wide when they saw who she stopped at.

“G-good morning…” Sora muttered.

Nanami gave a bright, blinding smile.

“Good morning, Mr Prince!” She exclaimed, before leaning down and doing the unthinkable to everyone.

She kissed Sora square on the lips.
