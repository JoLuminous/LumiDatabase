FRACTALS

-Preface-

This story is heavily inspired by the 1844 Hans Christian Andersen fairy tale The Snow Queen. My intent when writing this was to make a more narrative-focused version of the original novel, as I felt there were parts of it that felt unnecessary or not connected. The biggest task I set to achieve with my writing was the fleshing out of the Snow Queen herself. While the original fairy tale is named after the antagonistic character, she hardly appears after her introduction, and doesn’t interact with anyone once Gerda reaches her castle. I wanted to make a more formidable snow queen. My story is also entirely original in its characterization and relationships, as it was not my goal to replace the original or claim that my version is superior. I have simply made a new story based on the characters and the world from Hans Christian Andersen’s fairy tale.

-Plot Synopsis-

14 year olds Greta and Kaye have been childhood friends for as long as they can remember. Their lives practically revolved around each other. They always played together, studied together, and ate together. The two seem to grow closer with every passing year. Kaye finds himself particularly falling in love with Greta. However, their peaceful days full of love and sunshine are broken when a massive snowstorm strikes, and Kaye ends up caught in the crossfire. When Greta and her family rescue him and bring him inside, he becomes really aggressive. Greta immediately notices his pearly blue eyes. While trying her best to comfort him, he calls her ugly. While alone outside one day, Kaye is approached by a queen who seems to be made of snow and ice.. She encourages him to board a golden sleigh helmed by crystal reindeer. Kaye is swept away while Greta isn’t looking. When she finds that her dear friend has vanished, she decides to sneak away from her hometown to find him. Who is this mysterious queen of snow and ice? And why has Greta’s friend turned against her? Will Greta be able to dislodge the fractals of darkness plaguing his heart?

Prologue

Many centuries ago, before the time of you and I, the world was nothing but a green prairie.

The stars flowed like lights, clear, not obscured by skyscrapers, and the air was clean, not full of pollutants.

This is the world of our story. A world of fantasy and magic. Monsters and knights. Kings and queens.

And in a faraway part of this land, a village sat at the bottom of a rocky hill. This village contained no people, no this was a goblin village.

The goblins were a foul species, they sought no companionship with others, their only allies were themselves. It was common for goblins to value the people of the village they live in, meaning outsiders were not tolerated or liked in any way.

At this very moment, an outsider was walking through the gates of this village. She was not a goblin, but to call her human would be blasphemous. No, she was like a god, but more a demon.

Her hair was white like snow, her skin was pale, but glowed in the midday sun. Her footsteps were light and made no impression.

It was why the guards of the village only noticed her after several minutes.

Before the mysterious young woman could utter another breath, a flurry of arrows came flying at her, with speed that a normal person couldn’t compete with.

But this young woman was no mere human.

With inhuman reflexes, the young woman pulled out two ornate swords and struck all of the arrows down like it was nothing.

Suddenly, goblins ran out from various directions, wielding large swords. The young woman grins.

A large battle ensues, though it would probably be more accurate to describe it as a slaughter. The goblins stood no chance against this outsider, she killed all of them without even flinching or stopping to look.

Once she was done with all of the soldiers that came her way, she then made her way to every house and slaughtered every goblin she saw, no matter if it was a female, male, or even child.

After an hour, the village was quiet, an eerie aura in the air. The mysterious woman took no time to observe this, however. She simply continued on her way, stopping just outside a large black castle on the edge of a cliff that overlooked the village.

“Impressive…” The construction of the castle was infinitely more well rounded than the other buildings in the village.

The goblins that lived here were older and more intelligent than the goblins in the village, so naturally they protected themselves in a smarter way. Being secluded, they also built many things for their own use and amusement.

But the mysterious young woman who stood outside their doors didn’t care about that.

She sliced through the giant wooden doors blocking her way and stepped into the grand hall of the goblins.

There, she saw her prize.

In the middle of the hall was an ornate mirror, adorned with diamonds and crystals. The mysterious woman cared nothing for their construction, however.

The goblins, smart as they are, saw the woman and something seemed to click in their minds.

“You…the Snow Queen!”

Yes, this young woman was known by many as “the Snow Queen”, a feared figure throughout the land.

The goblins knew her, but figured that maybe she wanted to request their help.

“Are you here to request our assistance? I assure you that our weapons making skills are unmatched by many—”

The goblin king couldn’t finish his statement, as the queen sliced his neck clean open.

She didn’t want their help, nor their weapons.

The other goblins run for the exit in fear, but the queen kills all of them without effort.

Having succeeded in killing all of the goblins in the village, the queen approaches her prize: the mirror.

It was beautifully made, the queen rubbed her fingers along its surface, marveling at the craftsmanship. But she didn’t care for the mirror at all, she only wanted what it contained.

With a swipe of her sword, the mirror shattered into millions of small pieces. The snow queen collected the pieces into a jar and then strode off, satisfied with her task.

I: Her Lovely Singing Voice

If you were to ask a guy what he devotes most of his dedication towards, what would his answer be?

Probably girls. The hair, the scent, the lovely demeanor. You can’t go wrong with girls.

Every guy (presumably) would give up everything if it means getting with a nice girl.

15 year old Kaye was one such guy. He has a lovely friend named Greta, and she was one of the nicest, purest girls you could ever hope to meet.

Her eyes were like purple jewels, and her hair was golden like the purest honey. She wasn’t the most beautiful girl in the world, there were plenty others who might be more fit for that title, but she was cute in her own right.

But even with her looks, which Kaye found wonderful, there was something above it all that made Greta simply the best girl: her singing voice.

Greta loved to sing, but she only really did it in private due to embarrassment. Kaye has asked her several times to sing for him, but she never relents.

He has to settle for secretly listening behind Greta’s back. She has a beautiful voice, not musician level, but also nice and calm in a way that makes Kaye’s heart soften every time.

As far as Kaye was concerned, he would do anything to protect that wonderful voice. But even if Greta wasn’t a good singer, Kaye would undoubtedly be by her side in a heartbeat.

In the years that Kaye had known her, Greta had always been very fragile and emotionally vulnerable. There were many tragic events in the past that caused great distress within her, and so Kaye felt obligated to keep her steady.

Probably the worst event in Greta’s life was the death of her dear mother. Greta always loved her mother, they were an unbreakable pair, and more than just relatives, they were friends.

But life works in cruel ways.

One fateful year, a rapid epidemic spread throughout Kaye and Greta’s village, killing many in its path. Greta’s mother was one such victim.

Her death hit hard, not just for Greta, but for the entire village as a whole. Greta’s mother was just that influential and beloved.

Unfortunately, there was very little that Kaye could do for his dear friend. He sought to comfort her, but also felt woefully unqualified to do so.

After all, what had he lost? His parents were still alive.

Kaye decided it would be better to say nothing, rather than potentially hurting her with his words. He ended up regretting this decision.

The day of Greta’s mother’s funeral, Kaye found his entire life crumbling into pieces. He would never forget the crushing sight of Greta, staring dead-eyed at her mother’s coffin.

Such features never belonged on the girl’s face, and yet…

Kaye had let it happen. He blamed himself. He hated himself. He considered himself an idiot for letting things be this way.

All this time, he had ignored Greta’s pain, worrying for basically no reason, letting her cry, suffer, be miserable, and for what? Saving his own pride?  

Kaye declared that he would never let Greta feel that sad ever again.

Two days after the funeral, he approached his depressed friend. She had been sitting at her bedroom window for hours since her mother’s death, and no one could get her to do anything different.

Kaye slowly approached her slumped figure and got on his knees, placing a hand on her shoulder.

“Greta.”

He whispers. No response.

“Greta, look at me.”

Kaye pleads, reaching out and turning her head towards him. He gasps.

Greta’s eyes…they were red with tears. Dried tracks lined her face, and a seemingly unbreakable frown was etched across her once exuberant features.

“Have you been eating, Greta?”

Kaye asks worriedly.

Greta says nothing.

Suddenly, she forcefully wraps her arms around Kaye, moaning woefully.

“Greta…I hate to see you like this.”

Kaye admits.

“I want to take you somewhere, is that okay?”

Greta pauses for a second and then nods slowly.

Kaye smiles and then grabs Greta by the hand. He leads her outside and they quietly walk through town.

She immediately starts to cower under the words of the townsfolk, who loudly talk with comments of pity. Kaye squeezes her hand and takes her forward.

Greta looks up at him and slightly smiles, before it disappears instantly.

After a few minutes of walking, they finally arrive at their destination. Greta gasps at what she sees.

A massive monument had been erected atop a grassy hill outside the village. Flowers, gifts, candy, and other offerings had been placed around it, along with thousands of kind letters.

Tears started to fall from Greta’s eyes.

“T-this…”

Kaye smiles at her.

“Everyone loved your mother, Greta. There are others here who share your pain and sadness. I brought you here because I wanted you to understand that. You are not alone.”

Greta held a hand over her mouth as tears continued to fall.

“T-thank you…”

Kaye nods.

“Of course. But Greta…”

Kaye pulls her into a hug.

“I think your mother would have wanted you to smile. It’s okay to cry, but it’s better to be happy for the life she lived, than sad for what you miss.”

Greta’s eyes go wide.

“It…it might take me a while to be happy again…”

She admits.

Kaye places a hand on the top of her head and rubs her hair softly.

“That’s fine. I’ll be here to help you.”

Greta looks up at him.

“Y-you’ll be here?”

Kaye nods.

“I’ll be here.”

Greta looks to the ground, and then buries her face in Kaye’s shirt.

“Let’s do our best…”

She mutters.

Kaye chuckles.

So began the days of Greta’s recuperation. Kaye did the best he could to cheer Greta up, even if that pushed his own limits. He took Greta on all sorts of adventures in town, showed her interesting things, and bought her cool trinkets.

Eventually, Greta was starting to smile again. Kaye was happy.

And yet…

Not one of those smiles seemed genuine. The poor girl was still hurt inside. No matter what Kaye did, he wouldn’t be able to fill the hole left by her mother’s death.

But Kaye didn’t stop trying. He played with her, joked with her, and encouraged her, for as long as necessary.

Greta was still sad, but her smiles were becoming more natural again, and she cried less. Then after that, she started singing.

That was when Kaye first fell in love with her.

Then, one bright sunny day, when Kaye and Greta were playing after school, something happened that hadn’t happened in a long time:

Greta smiled.

Brightly.

Brilliantly.

Happily.

And she said to her stupefied friend:

“Thanks for being by my side, Kaye.”

Kaye didn’t think he had ever seen Greta looking so beautiful until that day.

“Of course, what are friends for?”

He responded after a few seconds.

Greta giggles and then looks out into the sunny horizon.

It was a breathtaking sight.

No, not the sun.

Greta.

She was beautiful, it was like an oil painting, painted with the highest quality oils.

Her eyes were painted with a magnificent deep purple, like pristine fine jewels.

Her blonde hair was painted like gold, producing a wondrous sheen that was breathtaking.

“We’ll always be together, won’t we Kaye?”

Greta asks.

Kaye smiles and hugs her from behind.

“I would never think of leaving your side, not even for the largest diamond.”

Greta looks to the ground with a flush.

“I wish we could stay like this forever.”

She muses aloud.

“So do I…”

Kaye whispers to himself.

The sun then began to set upon the world, and the two returned to their homes.

Kaye knew he would never leave Greta’s side.

But what did Greta think about him?

In bed that night, Greta couldn’t get Kaye out of her head.

He had been with her through the toughest time in her life. He made her laugh, he showed her things she never would have thought of seeing.

Kaye wasn’t the only one who had fallen in love.

II: His Pearly Blue Eyes

The town of Wilfonde was the kind of place where if you did something big, everyone would know about it.

It was a quaint little town, situated away from the active areas of the kingdom, but for what they lacked in outside contact, they made up for in inner relations.

Everyone knows each other. If you were someone who just moved in, it would be plainly obvious. Why? Because living in this town requires knowing every single inhabitant by their looks.

You couldn’t get by if you didn’t.

In a happy corner of town, two houses sat adjacent to each other, connected by a handmade overpass.

This is where lovely Greta and amiable Kaye lived.

A makeshift balcony connected the windows to their bedrooms, giving them ample space to meet up when they wanted to.

And they met up often. Today was no exception.

A smiling Greta greeted her friend Kaye in the early morning, sitting next to him in a wooden rocking chair, a tea cup in her hand.

“Good morning.”

She says with a happy glee about her. Kaye turns to look at her.

“Yes it is.”

He says with a smile.

Greta giggles. Then she starts to hold herself, shaking a little in the morning cold.

“It’s…s-so cold out here…”

Kaye takes off his woolen scarf and wraps it around her.

“Is that better?”

He asks. Greta hides the flush in her cheeks as she mutters:

“Yes it is. Thank you.”

Kaye nods in response and looks out into the morning sky once again.

“So…will you sing for me today?”

He asks, grinning.

Greta groans.

“You always ask this! My answer isn’t changing!”

Kaye shakes his head in exasperation.

“Is it really that embarrassing?”

Greta gulps down her entire tea cup and slams it down on a side table.

“Trust me, if I sang for you, you’d probably hate the experience.”

Kaye sighs.

“You shouldn’t be so hard on yourself.”

He says, slightly upset.

“Yeah, yeah…”

Greta looks to the ground with a blush.

They’ve had this conversation before about her singing.

Kaye seemed to ask her the same question every single day, but Greta would never relent for as long as she lived.

Why? Because she truly was embarrassed.

Most of her songs are about Kaye, so naturally, singing to him would expose everything she wants to hide.

Her feelings for Kaye had developed ever since the death of her mother, and yet she could never find the courage to tell him.

“So have you thought about the future, yet?”

Kaye suddenly asks, pulling Greta out of her thoughts.

“Huh?”

She asks in surprise.

“Yesterday we talked about the future in class, so I was just curious if you’ve thought about it.”

Kaye explains.

Greta frowns.

“Oh, well…I haven’t really considered anything, yet…”

Kaye’s eyes go wide, and then he turns away from her awkwardly.

“Ah, I see…”

 

Greta tilts her head.

“Is something wrong?”

She asks curiously.

“Well–”

Suddenly, the door to the balcony opens, cutting Kaye off.

“Greta, Kaye, breakfast is ready.”

Greta’s father says.

“Oh, we’ll be right down!”

Greta exclaims, surprised.

Kaye looks like he wants to say something else, but decides to leave it for later.

After having a hearty breakfast of bacon and eggs, prepared by Greta’s grandmother, Kaye and Greta left for school.

The Wilfonde school building was located on the opposite side of town, meaning the two friends had to always get up early in order to walk all the way there.

Kaye and Greta didn’t have any particular feelings about school, but they consistently had good grades, so it wasn’t like they were bored by it.

Today’s school day went like any other, until the final class, when the teacher decided to ask a certain question.

“Has anyone thought about their plans for the future? Having a good career is important for a successful life!”

Naturally, there wasn’t anyone who raised their hands in response to this question.

Well, other than Kaye.

“Ah, Kaye, so you’ve thought ahead. Would you mind telling us what you plan to do?”

Kaye takes a deep breath, noticing that Greta was staring at him with expectant eyes.

Feeling confident, he stands and says:

“I want to attend the academy in the capital.”

Several gasps filled the room, and Kaye could feel someone glaring daggers at him.

“That’s a big dream, Kaye. I’m impressed.”

The teacher says.

Everyone seemed to be in admiration of Kaye for his bold declaration, all except for Greta.

When school was over, and they walked home for the day, the air was tense.

“Kaye.”

Greta starts sharply. Kaye winces.

“Y-yeah?”

He nervously responds.

“You never mentioned this to me!”

Greta suddenly exclaims.

“Are you really planning to leave us behind? Are you seriously going to…leave me?”

She asks, her voice slightly breaking.

“This is what I have to do.”

Kaye responds, his anger now growing.

“In order to have a good job, I need to get better. The academy can give me that!”

He yells.

“But…you can still have a good life if you stay here!”

Greta yells in return.

“Life isn’t that easy, Greta!”

Kaye snaps.

“Oh, so you’re an expert on that front, then?”

Greta snaps back.

“Why are you so against this? What have you got to show? You don’t even know what you’re going to do after graduating school!”

Kaye exclaims.

“There are jobs here! There’s plenty to do! What reason is there for getting a higher education?”

Greta asks angrily.

“I just need to get better, okay?”

Kaye snaps.

“That’s stupid!”

Greta snaps back.

“No it isn’t.”

“Yes it is!”

“Is not!”

“Is so!”

“Is not!”

“IS SO!”

“IS NOT!”

“IS-”

Greta’s front door suddenly opens, and her father comes out with Kaye’s parents. They separate the two.

“What’s all the ruckus about out here?”

Greta’s father asks.

“Kaye wants to leave me behind!”

Greta exclaims.

“Greta won’t let me do anything for myself!”

Kaye exclaims in turn.

““HE/SHE IS BEING SO SELFISH!””

They yell in unison.

Their guardians sigh.

“You two, this is ridiculous. Let’s go inside and have dinner.”

Kaye’s father says in exasperation.

“But–”

Greta tries to say, but her father cuts her off.

“You heard the man, Greta. Let’s all calm down and go in, okay?”

Greta looks to the ground dejectedly and sighs.

“...okay…”

They all head inside for dinner.

And yet…

The air was still tense around the kitchen table.

Everyone could feel the animosity growing between Kaye and Greta, but felt helpless to quell it.

It was probably the quietest dinner they ever had. The only sounds heard in the little kitchen were the clinking of forks and the chewing of food.

It was like a ticking time bomb. At any moment, the fuse could blow, and the peaceful silence would be shredded in an instant.

Finally giving the room some sound, Greta spoke up:

“So, you aren’t serious, are you?”

The tenseness of the moment grew tenfold.

“Haven’t we just had this discussion?”

Kaye asked with an annoyed glare.

Greta responded in turn with her own glare.

“Well, you still haven’t explained why this is so necessary.”

She snaps.

“You’re so attached to this village. When are you going to start thinking about the broader picture?”

Kaye snaps back.

“You act like this village hasn’t given us so much in the past! Do those memories mean nothing to you?”

Greta asks, her anger rising.

“There’s more to life than just sitting on the past! I want to see the world, experience things I haven’t seen before! You want to be trapped in a bubble all your life?”

Kaye asks, slamming his fork on the table.

“Children, children, let’s calm down—”

Greta’s grandmother is interrupted, as Greta suddenly stands up in fury.

“I’ll calm down when he stops with this selfish plan of his!”

Now Kaye stands up, his fury just as strong as hers.

“Selfish? You’re the selfish one! When are you going to stop living in my shadow and grow the fuck up? You can’t just spend your entire life mourning over your dead mother!”

Silence filled the room. Tears started to build in Greta’s eyes.

“I…I get it now. You never cared about me, huh? Little ol’ Greta was just a mild amusement for you. I suppose you never even wanted to stay by my side, then?”

She says, more sad than angry.

“Greta, I…I’m sorry, it was the heat of the moment, and—”

“Don’t say another word. I’m going to bed.”

Greta saunters off to her room as Kaye watches, hopeless.

“I…why did I say that?”

Kaye asks guiltily, laying his head down on the table.

“Kaye, Greta didn’t mean any harm. She just wants the best for you. When you’ve grown up in this sheltered environment, it’s only natural to be worried when you’re going out into the world.”

“I know that…of course I know that…but…I just wanted to give Greta the life she wants. There’s no way I can support her when I don’t have the education and skills to do so…”

Kaye says quietly.

Greta’s grandmother smiles.

“Then you should tell her that.”

Kaye’s eyes go wide, and then he looks to the ground.

“Alright…”

He was hesitant, but this was for the best. He couldn’t let things end with those horrible comments he said.

Kaye walked up the creaky stairs and slipped into Greta’s bedroom, taking care not to disturb her.

He quietly approaches her bed and stares down at her blanket-covered form.

“Greta.”

He calls softly. No response.

Kaye figures that she is already asleep, so he prepares to walk out, but then stops.

“Greta, I’m sorry, for earlier. I know you loved your mother, and I shouldn’t have used that fact for my hurtful comments…”

No response. Kaye knows his efforts are fruitless, but continues anyway.

“The reason I’m leaving…I told you it was to further my own education, but…there is another reason for it.”

Kaye takes a deep breath.

“The truth is…I’m thinking of marrying you…in the future, of course. I want to travel outside the village so I can better myself…then when I’m stronger, I can support you like a proper man should.”

Kaye’s face was a little red at this point. Greta still hadn’t responded to his words, but she was secretly awake the whole time. Her face was red as well.

Kaye…

“I know you can’t hear a word I’m saying, because you’re asleep, but…I just wanted to get this off my chest.”

Greta smiles to herself.

I can’t believe I doubted him. He cares so much about me…

“Kaye…”

She mutters.

Kaye’s eyes go wide.

“Huh? You’re awake?!”

He asks nervously.

Greta nods.

“Thank you. I’m sorry, too.”

Kaye didn’t know what to say at first, but then he smiled.

“I guess we’re both major idiots, huh?”

Greta chuckles.

“You’re not wrong.”

They laughed for a bit

“So…goodnight, then?”

Kaye asks after a while.  

Greta smiles.

“Yeah. Goodnight.”

Kaye waves, and then heads for his own bedroom.

Greta watched him leave, feeling slightly lonely.

If Kaye does leave me…would I be able to handle it?

Greta can’t bring herself to answer the painful question.

She went out onto the balcony, but found no one. Where was Kaye? Usually he woke up before her.

Maybe he’s still asleep?

Greta walks over to Kaye’s bedroom and peeks through one of his windows.

Kaye wasn’t there either.

Suddenly, Greta felt something on her arm. She looked at it but there was only a little bit of water.

Then she looks up and sees little white specks of ice falling from the sky.

It was snowing!

“Snow?”

Greta says, feeling sad.

If it was snowing, it wasn’t a school day, so Greta was going to make the most of it by sleeping in. She never liked waking up early.

Greta asked aloud.

“Grandma! Grandma! Is Kaye going to be okay? He can’t make it in that storm, can he?”

“I don’t know, Greta. He may come home, he may not. I think if you pray for him, he might just make it.”

Greta doesn’t fully believe this, but she doesn’t really have a choice, so she sits down in a chair near the fireplace and prays.

Please god…I don’t want to lose someone close to me again. Please bring Kaye back safe.

All Greta could do was hope and pray.

“Greta.”

“Kaye, w-what happened to your eyes?”

“Get off of me, you disgusting wench! Who do you think you are?!”

III: Her Enchanting Sleigh

In her 16 years of living, Greta came to know a few things.

She knew the sun came up in the morning, and the moon rose in the night.

She was aware that time was ongoing, and anything could happen.

She had come to terms with the fact that death was inevitable, and sometimes unexpected.

She even understood that her world was infinitely smaller than others, and that her selfishness shouldn't get in the way of what’s right.

But the thing Greta knew most of all was that Kaye would never, ever, betray her. Kaye’s compassion was a facet she believed was always true, never disputed, she didn’t even have doubts about it.

“Kaye…wouldn’t be mean to me, would he?”

No, Kaye was the nicest person Greta had ever met. Why would that change?

Why…?

That question was one that Greta had been asking herself for hours since the day Kaye woke up from his snowstorm injuries.

When he uttered the word “wench”, Greta could not find any words to say.

VI: Her Prickly Roses

“Kaye!”

Greta awoke with a start, finding herself in an unfamiliar setting. At first she was confused, but then she remembered yesterday, and how the hooded figure helped her get some lodgings.

“Let’s have a talk.”

“So we discussed the Rotted Elms yesterday. That place isn’t for the faint of heart. I prepared this map to guide you.”

“Thank you. This will help a lot.”

Greta says. She stores it in her backpack.

“Now, before you go, I want to warn you about this path you’re taking. The Rotted Elms are a wretched place for even the most experienced adventurers, and I recommend you think long and hard before trying to go in there. Also, don’t ignore the warnings on the map. They’re there for a reason.”

Greta found herself walking through farmlands, valleys, even large meadows of beautiful flowers. But she didn’t stop. She kept going, determined to find her way.

Greta observed the map, noticing a note that reads:

“Don’t take the right path. Roses lay there.”

She doesn’t understand.

“Roses lay there?”

Greta asks aloud.

“What does that mean?”

“It shouldn’t make a difference, right?”

After a few minutes of deliberation, and enjoying a quick snack, Greta made the final decision to do just that.

The right path seems pretty normal at first. Old trees surrounded her, full of ancient history. Mysterious creatures shimmied through the shrubbery at her feet, slightly jostling her.

As Greta pushed through the underbrush, she found herself standing on the threshold of a seemingly hidden grotto.

It was like the trees had conspired to create a secret quiet sanctuary, forming a circle, with branches that reached towards the sky.

Soft moss-covered ground cushioned her feet as she walked forward into the grotto.

It was at that moment when Greta’s gaze fell upon a pristine white cottage nestled at the center of the clearing, like an ethereal structure erected by god. Its walls shined, making it seem like it didn’t belong there, and Greta was absolutely surprised by its sudden appearance.

“How quaint…”

She muttered.

A mixture of awe and trepidation filled her mind as she continued forward, cautiously approaching the house, with the expectation that anything could be living there.

“Turn back!”

A small voice suddenly said, stopping her in her tracks.

“Don’t go!”

Another small voice said, but Greta didn’t listen.

“Turn back!”

The small voice said again, but Greta kept walking, as if in a trance.  

A weird aura seemed to flow through the house’s walls, filling her with a strange feeling she couldn’t explain.

Greta’s eyes then widened in surprise, as she spotted a woman standing at the far end of the room she was in.

“I’ve been expecting you, little traveler. You seem lost.”

She says.

“Who are you?”

Greta asked, backing up slightly.

“Why, my name is Miss Myrtle, I’m the old witch of these woods! It brings me such joy to play with young girls like you!”

“Miss…Myrtle…”

Greta repeated.

Her instincts were screaming madly at her to flee, but a queer curiosity kept her rooted to the spot.

“Flee!” “Flee!”

The small voices cried again, but this time Greta was too mesmerized to even consider them.

The air in the room was growing heavy, laden with a scent that reminded her of blooming flowers, sweet yet sickeningly intense and overwhelming.

“Come with me, dear child. For now that you have arrived, I must show you my wonderful garden!”

Says Miss Myrtle.

“Don’t do it!”

A small voice cried.

Greta tilted her head, she opened her mouth to speak, but words seemed to be forced out of her throat against her better judgment.

“...okay…Miss…Myrtle…”

She muttered, in a trance-like state.

The old woman smiled, taking her by the hand. Greta let herself be led along, not even really knowing what she was doing.

Miss Myrtle led her to a large trough at the end of the garden, where three large red-orange roses were blooming brilliantly, radiating an enticing aroma that made Greta want to get closer to it.

Just then, all at once, a flurry of small voices invaded her ears.

“Stop, girl!”

“Don’t touch the roses!”

“Turn back!”

“Fly away from here!”

“Turn back!”

 

“Turn back!”

“Turn back!”

“Turn back!”

“What’s the matter, child?”

Greta doesn’t know how to respond, but she can see her surroundings now, and didn’t think going further was a good idea. That was when she noticed something terrifying: the roses were moving. They were breathing.

She was very wary now.

“I…I don’t think I want to see your roses anymore, Miss Myrtle. I have to go find my friend, Kaye.”

She says, releasing her grip on the old woman’s hand. Miss Myrtle frowns at her, a twitch in her eyes.

“My child, don’t you like the sight of my roses? They’re simply beautiful. Your friend Kaye is probably dead already. Stay here with me, and you’ll be safe for the rest of your life!”

“Um, I can’t stay here!”

“DON’T RUN FROM ME!”

“You should stay here, where the world is beautiful! My roses are better than anything in the outer world!”

Miss Myrtle cries hysterically.

Greta is losing the battle, her hands in pain from how hard she was grasping. What could she do to get this old woman off of her?

“Your knife!”

A small voice cries.

“Use your knife!”

Greta suddenly remembers. She briefly stops struggling, lulling Miss Myrtle into a false sense of security.

“You’ve stopped resisting! Finally you understand the beauty of–”

SKKRT!

Using the opportunity to her advantage, Greta had pulled the hooded figure’s knife from her scabbard and plunged it into the old woman’s stomach.

“Agh! You foul, miserable, BRAT!”

She cries, stumbling backward into one of the rose troughs due to her injury.

“When I get my hands on you, I’m going to– AH!”

“Let go of me! I am your creator! No!”

Greta winces as the old woman is lifted into the air by the slimy tentacles, before being swallowed alive by her own monstrous roses.

Greta runs back into the house and out the front door in her attempt to get as far away as possible.

She looks down at her knife-wielding hand, which was bloody and shaking from when she stabbed Miss Myrtle. The sight of it made her feel squeamish. She hoped she could find a stream to wash her hands in.

She continues down the path she came, glad to still be alive.

Greta wanted to thank those small voices she heard, but at the moment she still had no idea where they originated from.

“I don’t know where you are…”

She starts,

“But thank you for saving me.”

She finishes aloud.

“You’re welcome!”

VII: Her Forest Fears

Walking along the leftward path, Greta wondered if she was truly up for the tribulations of adventure.

It had been an hour since she departed from the old woman’s cottage, and so far everything seemed calm. However, Greta’s heart was anything but.

She could still remember the feeling of stabbing a living human being, and it was affecting her to a terrible degree.

Greta never thought she would end up killing someone, and wished she didn’t have to. The true dangers of her journey were starting to reveal themselves, and now she knew she couldn’t take things lightly anymore.

She shakily lifts her hand, staring soberly at the now dry blood. Would she have to see more, if she continued on this dreadful path?

Greta frowned, wiping her face before her tears could fall.

She just had to accept it. This was what she chose. If she didn’t, she would probably never see Kaye again.

“Anything…for Kaye…?”

Could she really do anything for him?

Greta shook her head, trying to get those awful thoughts out of her mind before they consumed her. She couldn’t turn back now.

Building her confidence as much as she could, she looks ahead with determination. Her eyes go wide.

Greta’s long trek had led her to the end of a winding path, revealing a thick, dense forest. The once vibrant and lush foliage now appeared sickly, with gnarled and twisted branches that seemed rotted at the core.

The sky above was dark and foreboding, much like the forest itself, and the trees seemed to move like they were truly alive.

Directly in front of her, arching trees seemed to create a natural, eerie gateway, beckoning her forth. The air of the surroundings also seemed to be drawing her in, as if it was calling her name in the winds.

Greta took a hesitant step forward, weary from her previous experience. All at once, a thick haze fell over her vision. She was uncertain, scared, nervous, but she shakily steeled herself and stepped into the decaying woods.

The moss at her feet was thicker now, yet also dead at the same time, and she couldn’t make out her shoes through it. The earth beneath was damp, wet, it felt like she would sink straight through if she didn’t keep walking.

With each step she made, whispered voices flew amongst the trees. Shadows danced and swirled around her, compounding the haziness in her eyes even more so, until reality itself seemed distorted.

Greta didn’t know what it was, but something in this forest seemed to be telling her that she wasn’t wanted. Suddenly, with a rustling of dead leaves and the shrill of strings, large flying beetles burst from the trees, startling her to the point of falling.

“Ah! Get away!”

She cried, swatting them with her hands, as they tried to gnaw at her in turn. One of the bugs snips at her middle finger, leaving a grotesque hole of ooze and blood.

“Eeek!”

Greta held her hand in pain as she screamed, sending the gross beetles scurrying away in fear. Something was in there now, but she didn’t know what it was. Stinging pain shot up each time she tried to move it.

“Mhn…it hurts…”

She muttered, weakly walking through the damp, rotted forest with a frown on her face. Stumbling along what seemed like the right path, mischievous voices raced past her ears.

“Pathetic little girl!”

“You’ve wandered too far!”

“You’ll be dead within the hour!”

The voices giggled in amusement at her pain and suffering, and Greta could only whimper and muster through it, hoping they’d go away.

She finds herself in a large canopy, the trees overhead seeming to create a ceiling of rotting moss and shrubbery. The sun wasn’t visible at all, and when she turned around, she found that her surroundings were very unfamiliar.

Paths extended in various directions, all looking identical to each other, and Greta couldn’t tell which one she used or where to go next.

“Where’s…the path?”

She whispers, her eyes growing tireder by the second.

The mischievous voices giggled at her.

“It’s that way!”

“No you fool, I believe it’s the other way!”

“No, I think it’s this way!”

“What way is that?”

“This way!”

“Oh, of course, that way!”

Their words were confusing, deliberately so, and Greta found she couldn’t understand anything or anyone. Where to go? Where to go?

ROOOAR!

Suddenly, a loud roar rings out between the trees, causing the ground to visibly shake. Greta goes pale, rooted to the spot in fear. She wants to scream, but nothing comes out.

A large black mass bursts through several nearby trees, sending shreds of wood and earth flying in various directions. Sharp, pointed teeth dripping with saliva face her, protruding from the mouth of a large ferocious panther. Its fur was as black as night, its eyes were as red as death.

Greta backs up slowly, trying not to antagonize it, but it leaps forth in its animalistic aggression. She jumps behind a fallen tree log, shaking as she does so. She had never been so scared in her entire life, as the panther stalked the area for her presence.

She pulls out her knife, holding it for dear life, as the panther slowly approaches her position. Suddenly, it leaps towards her, roaring as it does so.

Greta puts her head between her legs, causing the panther to land behind her. She turns around just as it prepares to attack her once again.

Within the course of a few seconds, she shakily drives her knife into the top of the panther's skull, but at the same time, she feels a stinging, blaring pain in her body and groans agonizingly.

The panther scurries off in a belligerent state, taking her knife with it. Even if it died nearby, Greta was in too much pain to try and look for it.

She falls to her knees and digs in her backpack for something to stop the bleeding. She grabs an old shirt and wraps it around her in an amateur way.

“Ow…ow!”

She hisses, the aging cloth feeling horribly rough against her skin.

Taking a much needed break, she also procures some stale porridge from the outpost, eating all of it despite the awful taste.

“Gross…”

She mutters with a grimace.

Greta looks for something to get rid of the taste and finds her water cask. She opens it and tries to quench her thirst, but nothing comes out. She peers inside with a confused look.

“Where did it all go?”

She asks aloud.

She was so thirsty, and the porridge didn’t help with that. She wearily stands up and goes down a random path, hoping for luck.

Deeper and deeper she went into the forest, the darker and darker it became. No matter how much Greta looked, the sun was no longer visible by her eyes.

Her legs were starting to grow tired, and after a while, she had no idea how much time was passing.

In a bout of good luck, she suddenly stumbles upon a babbling brook, its crystal clear waters looking delicious to a massively thirsty Greta.

She quickly leans down and cups her hands, taking several large sips of water. Her body was starting to feel light again, and Greta breathed a sigh of relief at this break from all the madness.

But in this cursed forest, there was unfortunately no such thing as a “break”.

For soon after she drank that delicious water, Greta felt hungry once more. She didn’t think much of it at first, consuming a piece of bread nonchalantly at the feeling.

But then it kept happening.

Ever so often, Greta would find herself wanting food again, and after a while, it got to the point where she was always hungry, no matter what she ate.

Her body was too light. She couldn’t feel the taste of food and she couldn’t feel the release of hunger. Then she felt thirsty, but the water of the brook couldn’t quench her at all.

Greta was growing miserable, lonely, sad, and discouraged.

“I’m thirsty…”

“I’m hungry…”

She whined.

The mysterious voices began to giggle again.

“You’re so stupid!”

One of them says,

“You can’t drink the sacred water! What a fool!”

Another teases.

“Shut up!”

Greta cried in annoyance. She didn’t need this, not right now.

Hours…

Or was it days?

Months?

Some amount of time passed since Greta drank from the brook and experienced true miserableness.

She herself knew not how long she had been in this forest, but it didn’t really matter to her anyway.

She had stopped counting the days since her departure a while ago…

But no matter how much time elapsed, Greta was in a state of weakness.

Every muscle in her body felt sore, even moving her arms was painful.

Greta’s legs were on the brink of failing her, the food and little drink she had saved up was gone, devoured in an attempt to satiate her gnawing appetite and thirst.

At this point, her clothes were also filthy and disgusting. Greta could feel that her undergarments and pants were soaked with sweat and she was so weak that she even pissed herself a few times.

It was just too difficult to crouch and remove clothing for something like using the bathroom. Greta’s dignity no longer mattered.

Suddenly, a loud piercing screeching sound reaches her ears.

SCREEEEE!

Bursting from the trees was a monstrous, pitch black colored, red eyed vulture, its feathers bathed in fire and smoke.

“Vul…ture…”

Greta mutters weakly, falling to her knees from the sight of it, and all at once, everything that came before hit her like a rock. She couldn’t do it. She gave up. She didn’t want to fight back anymore. Not if it meant nothing.

“Ah…I…can’t…so…weak…”

She just sat, staring at the monster with sad, distraught eyes. Her thoughts went to Kaye, to her father, to her grandma, and to her late mother.

Would she never see her family again? Would she finally join her mother on the other side?

Would she never be able to tell Kaye how much she loved him?

Greta couldn’t bring herself to stand. In fact, it was physically impossible for her body to move at this moment, she was just that weak.

The vulture, seeing her drop her guard, lunges forward with full force. She closes her eyes…

…but the impact doesn’t reach her, as a thick wooden spear comes flying through the trees, piercing the monster straight in its head.

Greta’s vision grows fuzzy as she looks around for the source of this attack. Suddenly a young brown-haired girl appears from the trees and runs over to her.

Her hair was braided and her features were soft. She analyzes Greta with a worried expression.

“Are you alright? That foul thing didn’t hurt you, did it?”

The girl asks.

Greta finds it hard to respond, her throat is aching for water, and she really wants something to eat. She just barely shakes her head, and then breathes out the words:

“Thank…you…”

The last bit of strength leaving her, Greta faints, as the tribal girl shakes her worriedly.
