HOLIDAYS AND LONELINESS: A BALLAD TO MY FATE

It was Christmas Eve, and the streets of Shimokitazawa were alive with bustling energy. That year, the city was graced with a rare snowfall. Families, couples, and friends walked together, their cheerful voices filling the air.

But amidst all the joy, Hitori Gotou stood as an obvious outlier. She didn’t fit into this sort of atmosphere, nor any kind of crowd-based environment, but this was far worse than any other occasion.

However, Hitori had been hopeful about this Christmas. She had envisioned spending it with her bandmates—even if it was just one of them. It was a longshot she knew, but she hoped they had come to understand her, to realize how much she needed their company.

Specifically, she thought about Kita Ikuyo. The cheerful vocalist of Kessoku Band brought a sickening brightness into Hitori’s quiet, anxious world. She changed her, making her feel at least a little more capable of doing things she wouldn’t normally attempt.

With Christmas approaching, this was no exception. Hitori had spent days mustering the courage to suggest something casual, like visiting a café together or even sharing Christmas cake—both things she would probably die doing.

But when she’d finally attempted to bring it up, when she had quietly mumbled, “What are your plans for Christmas?”, the answer was not what she had hoped for.

“Christmas? This year I’m planning to attend a party with my classmates.”

That had been two days ago. Hitori, crushed but too shy to admit any of the plans she had been thinking of, had merely nodded and waved it off.

Now, she walked alone through the busy streets, the thought of Kita laughing and chatting with her classmates weighing heavily on her chest. Her phone buzzed with a text from Nijika, the band’s drummer.

Nijika: “Merry Christmas, Bocchi-chan! Are you doing anything fun tonight?”

Hitori stared at the screen for a long time before typing back:

Hitori: “No plans… Merry Christmas.”

She put her phone away and kept walking. She didn’t really know where she was going, but eventually she found herself in the park. She sat down on a secluded bench, hiding herself to avoid seeing any disgustingly romantic couples.

She pulled her guitar case off her shoulders and unzipped it, looking down at her instrument with confliction. Maybe she could record a video for her Guitarhero channel?

But as she picked it up, Hitori decided to do something different. Instead of uploading a video, she started an impromptu livestream, setting up her phone to face her.

It was cold, and very snowy, but Hitori didn’t even seem to notice.

A steady influx of viewers poured in as she began to play various songs. Oddly enough, this was probably the largest number of viewers Hitori had ever seen on her channel. That was most likely due to the eye-catching title she used: HOLIDAYS AND LONELINESS: A BALLAD TO MY FATE.

At first, Hitori didn’t really pay any attention to her viewers. But then she looked up after playing several songs and noticed the amount of comments.

“I’m alone too.”

“We’re in the same boat, Guitarhero.”

“Loneliness is forever.”

“I listen to you play and I don’t feel so sad anymore.”

“This is a safe space for the lonely ones (me included).”

Hitori made a small smile.

“Everyone…”

As she began to play more songs for her comrades, she started sharing more details about her situation.

“I almost had a chance,” she said sadly. “But… I got scared, and… my friend… I think she prefers her classmates over me. She’s attending a Christmas party… but I never even heard about it until I asked her.”

“What a bad friend!”

“No, she’s not bad… she’s just… I guess she doesn’t really… understand me,” Hitori whispered.

“The extroverted ones are always the least understanding of people like us.”

Hitori nodded. “You’re right… maybe… but I want to believe.”

“Is this the first time she’s neglected you?”

“Well… she has… forgotten a date once. We were going to hang out and she… totally forgot about it.”

“So she has a habit of this!”

“No, that’s not true. She’s just… she told me it was an honest mistake,” Hitori said, though she hesitated. “Could it have been… a lie? Maybe she didn’t want to hang out with a water flea like me…”

“Don’t rely on her! She’s no good!”

“Yeah! Who would schedule a date and forget about it later? And with Guitarhero no less?”

“But… but she’s nice. She isn’t a terrible person,” Hitori said, shaking her head. “Everyone… what should I do?”

“Free yourself, Guitarhero!”

“You don’t need a friend like that!”

“I think you should tell her off! Put her in her place! She’s friends with Guitarhero and is wasting the opportunity!”

Hitori frowned. She didn’t know whether these messages were comforting or concerning. But either way, she just kept playing her guitar. At this point, a few people had come to watch her play from afar, though Hitori didn’t notice, deliberately so.

“I don’t think she meant to hurt me,” Hitori said after a while, addressing her audience. “She’s just… so bright, so full of energy. I’m the complete opposite. Maybe I’m just dragging her down.”

The chat erupted in protest.

“No way!”
“You’re not dragging anyone down!”

Hitori hesitated. “But she’s always been kind to me… Maybe I’m the one who doesn’t understand her.”

She looked down at her guitar, feeling the familiar weight of self-doubt settling in her chest. “Maybe I just… don’t belong anywhere.”

Meanwhile…

Nijika watched the livestream with a conflicted expression. At first she was surprised to see Hitori of all people streaming, but as she watched the interactions that took place and read the chat… she was becoming worried, and also a bit angry.

“This isn’t good,” she whispered. “Not good at all.”

Ryo appeared by her side with a nod. “Live streaming in the snow… that’s kind of rock isn’t it?”

“Not now, Ryo!” Nijika snapped, her voice tinged with frustration. “Don’t you see what’s happening here?”

Ryo tilted her head, her usual calm demeanor unshaken. “Yeah, I see it. Everyone’s really leaning into the whole ‘introverted loner’ thing. They’re eating it up.”

“That’s the problem!” Nijika exclaimed, her voice rising. “They’re not helping—they’re making it worse! Look at those comments—they’re just feeding her insecurities.” She leaned closer to the screen, her fingers gripping the edge of the desk. “Kita-chan probably didn’t mean to hurt her, but now Bocchi-chan’s convinced she’s… she’s not good enough.”

Ryo shrugged, unfazed. “I mean, she’s always kind of thought that, hasn’t she?”

“This is different!” Nijika said, exasperated. She let out a heavy sigh. “Seriously, what was Kita-chan thinking? Going to some party without even telling Bocchi-chan?”

Ryo crossed her arms, nodding thoughtfully. “Yeah, she does tend to get swept up with people. Kita’s got that shiny, popular thing going on.”

“That… idiot.” Nijika shook her head. “I’m calling her. She needs to hear exactly what I think about this.”

Kita sat in a crowded Karaoke room, singing with her classmates. She never hesitated to have fun with other people. It was just in her nature.

But a small voice in the back of her mind told her that she was missing something. She had noticed the way Hitori’s face dropped when she mentioned going to this party. At the same time, she didn’t think too much of it. Had she missed a key detail?

At that moment, she heard her phone ring. She checked it and saw that Nijika was calling.

“I’ve got a call,” Kita announced to the group, her tone light but with an underlying urgency. “I’ll be right back!”

She quickly excused herself, stepping into the hallway away from the noise. She pressed the phone to her ear, already feeling a bit apprehensive.

“Hello?”

“Kita-chan, you’re an idiot!” Nijika’s voice blared through the speaker, sharp and harsh.

Kita flinched, taken aback by the sudden outburst. “That’s not a nice way to start a phone call.”

“Good. You deserve it!” Nijika snapped, her voice tight with frustration. “Have you even looked at YouTube today? You better, you sociable dummy!”

Kita raised an eyebrow, momentarily confused by the outburst. “Why are you calling me just to yell? Fine, fine, I’ll check.”

Without waiting for another response, she pulled up her YouTube app and scrolled through the notifications. Her eyes froze when she saw the one that caught her attention: Guitarhero is live: HOLIDAYS AND LONELINESS: A BALLAD TO MY FATE.

Her heart dropped. "Hitori-chan is live?"

“She’s been live for the past hour!” Nijika’s voice crackled, full of urgency. “Go watch it, now. You’ll see what I mean.”

Kita’s brows furrowed. “But why would—?”

“Just do it!” Nijika interrupted, her voice harsh with worry. “Watch it, and maybe then you’ll get why I’m pissed off at you!”

With a frustrated sigh, Nijika hung up before Kita could respond.

Kita stared in confusion at the phone for a moment. She hadn’t expected Nijika to scream at her as soon as she answered, and she had no idea why the drummer was even angry in the first place.

She shook her head and then quickly clicked on the notification, her heart pounding.

The stream loaded, and the sight of Hitori sitting alone on a snow-covered bench filled Kita’s screen. Her friend’s pink coat was dusted with snow, her hands slow as she strummed her guitar.

The chat was scrolling rapidly, a mix of encouragement and misguided advice.

“I’m alone too.”
“Don’t rely on your so-called ‘friends!’”
“Forget her, Guitarhero. She doesn’t deserve you.”

Kita’s chest tightened. Were they… talking about her?

Hitori’s voice cracked slightly as she spoke between songs, her words heavy with emotion. “Maybe she doesn’t really care about me. Maybe I’m just… not interesting enough to keep her attention.”

Kita’s heart dropped.

“That’s not true, Hitori-chan!” she whispered to her phone, even though she knew her friend couldn’t hear her.

One comment caught her attention, making her stomach twist:

“You don’t need her. Free yourself, Guitarhero!”

“No, no, no…” Kita muttered under her breath. She wanted to shout at the chat, to defend herself, but there was no way to do that without making things worse.

Hitori began playing another song, her voice softer and more emotional than usual, and in Kita’s eyes, it was hauntingly beautiful. She always thought that Hitori should sing more often, but… why did she sound so sad?

Did Kita really hurt her? But it was just a simple party, and she had already agreed to go…

Then she saw a comment that opened her eyes. It was simple, but she couldn’t find any fault in what they said.

“If she was a true friend, she would have canceled those plans and hung out with you as soon as she saw how sad you were.”

Kita finally understood. It was natural for her to make plans with one group and turn down another; after all, she was friends with a lot of people, so of course she would end up only able to hang out with a few at a time.

But Hitori wasn't the same. For one, Kita really cared about her, more than she would admit. Second, Hitori was the kind of person who always felt insecure, she didn’t have many friends, so the ones she did have were special to her.

Hitori Gotou—the girl who hesitated when saying everything—had been confident enough to ask the extroverted Kita Ikuyo about Christmas.

And yet, she had treated Hitori like any of her other friends, forgetting that Hitori wasn’t like them.

Kita clenched her fists, a mix of determination and guilt welling up inside her. “I’m such an idiot,” she whispered, her voice breaking.

She didn’t hesitate any longer. Shoving her phone into her pocket, she hurried back into the karaoke room. Her classmates barely had time to register her abrupt return before she grabbed her coat and scarf.

“Kita-chan, where are you going?” one of them called out.

“Sorry! I have to go. Something… really important came up!” Kita replied, her voice firm despite the turmoil in her chest.

Ignoring their curious questions, Kita slipped out the door and into the cold winter air. She had no idea how to make things right, but it didn’t matter. She had to try for Hitori’s sake.

Hitori gave her final farewells as she ended the livestream, placing her guitar back in its case. Guitarhero had been a fun distraction, but she still couldn’t help feeling sad.

Where was Kita at that moment? Was she singing Christmas songs with her friends? Was she having fun without Hitori?

Despite ending the stream, Hitori couldn’t bring herself to leave the bench. It was like a bubble, a shield from the outer world, the outer turmoil. She took her guitar back out of the case and started playing again. She needed to be focused on something else… something else…

Her playing was good, as always, but this time it reflected her inner melancholy. Perhaps it was an epilogical ballad, a continuation of what had come before. But it lacked her usual rock style… even the most depressing rock songs contained powerful melodies. This one, however, was slow, methodical, and dreary on an entirely different level.

“Hitori-chan,” a voice called from above.

Hitori froze mid-strum, her head snapping up. Her wide eyes met Kita’s, and for a moment, neither of them spoke.

Part of her wanted to admonish Kita for abandoning her, for tossing her aside in order to go to the party. But Hitori couldn’t bring herself to do that. She wasn’t brave enough to say that. Her bravery had already been used up that month when she asked about Christmas a few days ago.

Kita wanted to say something too, a thousand apologies, offers for making up for her mistakes. But she was scared of saying the wrong thing. What could she say that wouldn’t upset Hitori further?

“K-Kita-san?” Hitori finally broke the silence, her voice barely above a whisper.

Kita offered a sheepish smile, holding up her phone. “I saw your livestream.”

Hitori blushed and turned away, her hands shaking. “Y-you were watching?”

Kita nodded, taking a hesitant step closer. “I… I heard everything, Hitori-chan. I’m sorry.”

Hitori looked down, her hands squeezing her guitar. “You don’t have to apologize, Kita-chan. It’s my fault. I’m just… not brave enough to say the things I want…”

“No,” Kita said, her voice firm but gentle. She sat down beside Hitori, feeling even more guilty upon realizing how cold it felt. “I’m the one who messed up. I always seem to… treat you like my other friends, Hitori-chan, but I know you aren’t like them. You’re special, Hitori-chan, and I should’ve realized how much courage it took for you to ask me about Christmas. I… I didn’t think about how my actions might affect you, and… I’m really, really sorry.”

Hitori glanced at her and then frowned. “But you always have fun with your classmates,” she murmured. “I didn’t want to ruin that for you…”

“You wouldn’t have ruined anything,” Kita said earnestly. “Have I told you how much you mean to me? You’re an important friend, Hitori-chan. If you’re feeling lonely or hurt because of me… I want to know. I want to be there for you.”

Hitori stared at her in disbelief, unsure of how to take these words. “You… care about me?”

“Of course I do.” Kita smiled warmly and, without hesitation, pulled Hitori into a hug. “And if you’ll let me, I’d like to spend the rest of Christmas Eve with you.”

Hitori shook her head frantically, imagining terrible scenes in which Kita’s fans killed and tortured her because she took away their precious idol.

“Y-you don’t have to—”

“I want to,” Kita said firmly. “I want to make it up to you, for Christmas, for all the times I’ve been careless… I want to start spending more time with you, Hitori-chan.”

Hitori wanted to object, but she couldn’t help slightly smiling at her words. Too bad for Hitori’s anxiety, but she was selfish when it came to Kita.

“Thank you, Kita-chan,” she whispered, trying desperately to hold back tears (it wasn’t working at all).

Kita smiled and brushed a tear from Hitori’s cheek. “You don’t need to thank me,” she said softly. Then, with a playful grin, she added, “Now, what do you say we go to karaoke? I know a great place.”

“Eh…” Hitori was prepared to throw out a dozen introverted excuses at this, but as she considered her true feelings on the matter, she realized… who cares? She was going to spend time alone with Kita and that was what she wanted. “O-okay. That might be… fun.”

Kita eyed her with a doubtful look. “Are you sure? I don’t want you to feel pressured…”

“I want to go,” Hitori said, in a rare showing of confidence. Wow, that was the second time that month. “I want… to spend Christmas with you, Kita-chan. It doesn’t matter where we go.”

“Hitori-chan…” Kita’s face turned red, and not just from the cold. Then she smiled again, this time wider than the last. “Then let’s spend the rest of tonight… together.”

Standing, Kita extended her hand, a silent invitation. Hitori hesitated only briefly before taking it, her fingers trembling as they intertwined with Kita’s. The two walked off into the slowly falling winter snow, hand in hand, their shared warmth chasing away the cold.
