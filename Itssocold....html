On a quiet Christmas eve night, the world was still, frozen in time, both metaphorically and literally.

Inside a modest apartment, everything seemed smaller, cozier. A kotatsu sat in the middle of the living room, having been set up in obvious haste. Beneath it, two close friends—Ayumi and Haruki—shared a pot of freshly brewed tea, enjoying a rare bit of peace together.

College had been merciless that semester, with exams, deadlines, and time-consuming jobs pulling them in opposing directions. Their shared Christmas tradition of sitting under the kotatsu and doing absolutely nothing had been a lifeline they clung to, a moment to relax and remember the friendship they developed ever since middle school.

“You’re here,” Ayumi replied airily, her hand waving as if to dismiss his presence altogether. “But you don’t count.”

“Good to know I rank somewhere below…” He paused, as though searching for the most insulting comparison. “…a plate of takoyaki?”

“A good plate of takoyaki,” she corrected, a grin tugging at her lips. She always loved teasing him—he always made it easy and never hesitated to play along.

“It’s so cold…” she muttered, ignoring his jab as she pulled the kotatsu blanket tighter around her.

“The kotatsu is on max heat,” he pointed out.

“I’m still freezing!” she exclaimed, casting a deliberately pitiful look his way. She even rubbed her arms for effect.
