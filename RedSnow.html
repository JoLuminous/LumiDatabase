Red Snow

“I was following the pack, all swallowed in their coats

With scarves of red tied 'round their throats

To keep their little heads from falling in the snow, and I turned 'round and there you go

And, Michael, you would fall and turn the white snow red as strawberries in the summertime.”

-Fleet Foxes, White Winter Hymnal

I

Maine is one US state that has a history of snow in the winter. In the heart of this state lay the town of Yarmouth. It certainly would be a miracle to visit this town during the holidays without encountering Christmas displays. You see, today was December 6th, 1989, and the citizens of Yarmouth were passionate about their Christmas decorations. Almost every year, they would put up all kinds of decorative pieces. Wreaths, garland, lights, blow ups, whatever you could think of, they would have. About 3 houses down the block, near the cul de sac at the end of the neighborhood, stood a mixed teenage girl who held a clear storage bin full of lights and greenery. The house was lacking in color, and the one thing it was needing was lots of decorating. That’s where this girl comes in. Phoebe Buris was a 16 year old sophomore who lived with her uncle at this house ever since her parents died. All of the decorations that were to adorn the house were to be placed by Phoebe. She had to do all of it herself, because her uncle didn’t really care about all of that. Phoebe dumps all of the decorations out onto the snow covered grass and sighs. She had purchased all of this stuff with money she saved up, but now it was looking like a massive undertaking. She wanted to just quickly throw something up on the house, but that’s not what her parents would have wanted. They always were adamant about Christmas, and she wanted to carry that tradition, despite them not being with her. Phoebe gathers the tangled up lights and tries to pull them apart, to no avail. She musters her strength and prys hard, causing her to stumble into the snow. She sighs again and throws the lights to the side. Suddenly, she began to hear footsteps, and looked up to see a group of girls staring her down. She glances at them angrily. “What do you want?” One of the girls held out her hand. Phoebe grabs it begrudgingly. “You need help here, don’t you?” She looks to the ground, flushed. “I can handle it...” “Come now. Let us help you.” The group of girls each take turns untangling the lights, until each set was separated. “What do you say?” Phoebe eyes them nervously, but then smiles. “Alright.” The other girls smile as well, and they get to work putting up the decorations. Some of the girls put the lights up, others put garland on the stairs inside the house, and Phoebe placed the tree in the living room with the help of another girl. Once everything was finished, Phoebe offered her gratitude. “Thank you so much.” “No problem. It’s the holidays after all.” Phoebe smiles, but then notices something that catches her eye. “Hey...what house is that up there?” She points to a house atop the hill at the end of the neighborhood which had only a few decorations. The girls look in that direction and suddenly go quiet. “That’s...um...well, a boy named Andrew lives there…” The other girls looked to the ground, and some of them had sad expressions. “What’s wrong?” “He doesn’t celebrate…” “Why is that?” Silence. No answer. “Hey, why is that? Why won’t you answer?” “We can’t elaborate further…” They walk off, leaving her to stand there, flabbergasted. She scratches her head, trying to comprehend the event that just occurred. One of her neighbors notices her standing there and shouts. “Hey, nice decorations!” She jumps, not expecting someone to speak. “Oh! Thank you! Say...that boy who lives in the house atop the hill there...why does he not celebrate?” “Well, um...have a nice day!” The neighbor quickly walks off. “Hey, you didn’t answer my question!” But he was already gone. “What is up with that house?” Over the next several hours, she goes from door to door, asking if anyone knows about Andrew on the hill. None of the people she speaks to give her a straight answer. By 8:00 at night, she lay face down in her bed, thinking about Andrew. The next day, she went to school with him and his house in her thoughts. One of her friends greeted her on the way into the classroom. “Hey Phoebe!” “Oh, Silia, I have a question you might be able to answer.” “Okay…” “So there’s this house up on the hill and someone told me that a boy named Andrew lives there...do you know anything about him?” “Hm...I don’t know a lot...I know he’s sitting right over there, though.” “Huh?!” She turns her head and sees a boy about her age writing something in a notebook. “That’s him?” “Yeah. I don’t know a whole lot, but...I  suggest you ask Dara. She’s a senior that has some sort of relation to him.” “Alright. Thanks, I guess.” As class started, Phoebe couldn’t help but glance at Andrew ever so often. She did feel like she was being sort of creepy, but at the same time, she was just a little curious. A few hours later, once other classes passed, Phoebe went to visit Dara, who was currently in the student body classroom. She knocks on the door and a student greets her. “What is it?” “Um, I want to speak to Dara…” The student stares for a few minutes and then gets Dara’s attention. She comes to the door. “Yes?” “I...I want to ask you about Andrew…” Dara’s eyes go wide, and she pulls Phoebe inside. She motions to a desk. “Sit here…” Phoebe sits down, and Dara sits next to her. She sighs. “So you want to know about Andrew…” “Yeah...why does he refuse to celebrate? Is there some cynical-” Dara stops her. “It’s not like that.” A tear rolls down her cheek. Phoebe looks at her, shocked at this. “Dara?” “Around 5 years ago...Andrew used to have the best house in this neighborhood. Him and his family always decorated the best out of anyone. They were good people. I should know; I was friends with his sister, Agatha. She was such a saint. She was so friendly, so kind. I’ll never meet another girl like her. Andrew and Agatha...they were super close...one could have sworn they were more than just siblings. They always decorated the house together and their skills were unmatched.” Another tear rolls down her cheek. “Apparently...their fireplace stopped working in november, so they got it replaced by a different company. This company, however...was not the best. The installation was faulty. One cold day, about 4 days before Christmas, Andrew went out to get new lights. With the new fireplace being badly installed, a fire started and Agatha perished in the blaze. The family was never the same, and I…” Dara wipes off a few more tears. “I still haven’t gotten over it…” She turns away, not wanting to say anymore. The other students in the room walk over to Dara and console her. Phoebe, frozen in shock, leaves the room quietly. She never felt so bad for anyone. He was almost in the same situation she was in. The rest of the school day, Phoebe could not stop thinking about Andrew. On one hand, she felt sympathy, but on the other hand, she felt they must have a connection with their similar backgrounds. That afternoon, she went up the hill towards Andrew’s house. In her hands was a box of chocolates. She reaches the front porch and prepares to knock, but then she sees him heading towards a cemetery on the left side of the hill. She places the box on one of the porch chairs and goes down after him. Reaching the cemetery, she hides behind a tree. She watches as he kneels at a tombstone and places a bouquet of roses in the snow. He places his hand on the stone. “I’m so sorry…” He then looks up to the sky. “God…” He starts to cry. “Why did you take her? Why didn’t you punish me instead?” Phoebe places a hand over her mouth. I shouldn’t be watching this, should I? She starts to walk away, but then she hears a faint humming, or maybe it was singing? She returns to the tree and peers over at Andrew. Andrew puts his down on Agatha’s tombstone and cries. It was quiet in the cemetery, but one could still hear his sobs. Phoebe had her back against the tree and she was crying as well. All of the memories of her parents and the things she felt when they died were coming back to her. She started to feel really guilty for even attempting to make him like her. I really shouldn’t be watching this...I should have left. Phoebe slowly walks backwards, when crack! She steps on a tree branch. Andrew looks over and sees her attempting to walk away. He runs over and grabs her by the collar. She looks at him with fear in her eyes. “No, no! Let go of me! I’m sorry!” “Why were you watching? Tell me!” “I...I just wanted to talk to you…” “If you thought you could confess or something, then you are mistaken! I don’t need anyone else. You don’t know how close me and my sister were. Nothing will replace that!” Phoebe suddenly realizes what he’s saying. “If...if you two were doing...those things, then her death must be some sort of punishment for you from God.” “Shut the hell up!” He tosses her to the ground and she runs away in fright. Phoebe heads back to her home and locks herself in her room. Her uncle inquires about her actions. “Phoebe, what the hell are you doing?” “N-nothing. I was just trying to get home before it got late…” “Right.” He sits back down on the couch. Phoebe sat on her bed, shaking from head to toe. She went to sleep with Andrew, Agatha, and her parents in her thoughts. It was really late when she finally fell into a peaceful slumber.

II

When the snow fell on December 21st, 1984, it was as light and soft as a cloud. It was cold, but not cold enough to freeze your face off. Along the town of Yarmouth, decorations lined the streets. The people of this town valued their decorations. Currently, if you were to look up on the hill, you would see two 12 year old kids, each climbing ladders that were dangerously close to tipping over. But their young faces were full of happiness and joy. 12 year old Andrew placed a set of lights on a high tree and climbed back down. He pressed a button, expecting to see their lights in action, but nothing happened. “Aggy!” “Yeah?” “These lights are dead!” “Are you kidding me? I just checked those!” She storms down the ladder and snatches the remote from his hands. “Let me see.” She presses the button. Again, nothing happened. She throws it to the ground. “Dang!” “I’ll go get some more. You should stay in the house until I get back.” “Huh? Why do I need to stay inside?” “If anything was to happen to you, like a ladder falling for example, then I wouldn’t be able to help.” She rolls her eyes and smiles. “I’ll be fine! Don’t worry!” Andrew sighs. “Alright.” He prepares to leave, but Agatha stops him. “Hm?” She looks to the ground bashfully. “B-be careful yourself.” Andrew smiles. “Of course.” They smile at each other for a few minutes, and then he heads off, walking carefully down the steep hill in the inner city. As he went through the busy streets, people waved and called out to him. He didn’t hesitate to acknowledge them. “Hey! How’s it going! Merry Christmas!” “Same to you!” After a few minutes, he finally reached his destination: a small CVS on the corner. Its glowing red letters were clearly visible amongst the grey backdrop of the city. In fact, they were even more accentuated by the red and green christmas lights that adorned the walls and windows. Truly, the only reason Andrew and his family frequented this store was because of how cheap their prices were. That, and they were very close by. Once inside, Andrew grabbed three boxes of lights and placed them on the counter. The cashier, who knew all of his family, smiled at him. “Back again?” “These lights seem to never be built to last.” She laughs. “If you want good lights then you should go to Wal-Mart.” “Well, it’s just easier to come here.” “I see.” She scans the lights and he hands her a ten dollar bill. She gives him his change, and he leaves. Andrew smiles to himself. He always liked being out in the city at this time of year. Just seeing the decorations put up on buildings and the cheer on peoples faces made him feel warm inside. As he continued on his way, he noticed a nearby meat shop which had a beautiful roast spinning on a fire. That might be good for Christmas dinner, he thought. He heads back into the neighborhood and heads on to the trail that led up the hill. Suddenly, he began to smell smoke. His eyes went wide and he quickly ran towards their driveway. He stops in his tracks, the CVS plastic bag falling from his hand. Flames had engulfed the entire house, and the two trees that sat outside the front were both completely destroyed. The innocent snow, which continued to fall around him, reflected the red-orangish hue of the fire, as he stood with a saddened expression. Suddenly, a horrible, terrible, unfortunate thought entered his head. His sister was still inside. He began to call out to her, hoping she could still be there, protected from the fire. “Sis! Agatha! Agatha...Aggy…” He fell on his knees, tears beginning to roll down face. As he sat in the snow crying in his despair, the sound of a car pulling up reached his ears. His parents departed the vehicle and quickly ran towards him. His mother puts her hands over her mouth. “No…No!” As Andrew held his head down, his father leaned down to face him with worry. “Where is your sister? Where is she?” Andrew said nothing. He just shook his head. His father realizes the terrible truth. “No...not Agatha!” He started to cry as well. They all sat there watching the house fall prey to the flames, the reddish hue reflecting off of their eyes. After a few minutes, and one sad, quiet phone call, the fire department arrived and put out the rest of the fire. Medical services entered the house following this, and they returned 20 minutes later. In their hands, they carried a gurney, and on it was a small figure wrapped in cloth. Andrew needed no one to tell him who it was. He slammed his fist on the ground. His parents couldn’t bring themselves to look. He walks over and tries to enter the ambulance, but they close the doors before he could. One of the medical personnel puts a hand on his shoulder. “I’m sorry. There’s not a high chance she could still be alive.” Andrew turns away from him. “Where was she?” “They found her in a boy’s bedroom.” Andrew’s eyes went wide, and tears started to well up again. “She...she locked herself in my room?” “It seems that way...they found her in the fetal position.” Andrew’s face began to contort into a mix of anger and sadness. She had so little time to live...and she spent her last moments thinking of me. He walks away, crying quietly. Over the next couple of days, the news hardly got better. Agatha couldn’t be saved; she died from asphyxiation. Her funeral and burial was held on December 25th. Though they could have rebuilt their house relatively soon, Andrew couldn’t go near it without breaking down. The rest of the family spent December at his grandparents’ house, and the construction was delayed to the summer of the following year. It was really quiet, and no one attempted to celebrate. They still had Christmas dinner, but it was the dreariest dinner ever. They were able to recover all of the presents, but the usual cheer of opening them was completely lost. They were constantly reminded of the fire with every gift, as the wrapping paper was blackened and faded. Andrew couldn’t bring himself to open any gifts his sister had given him. Once time had passed, they returned to their newly rebuilt home in silence. One of the first things Andrew did was place the gifts his sister had gotten him in the back of his closet. All of his sister’s belongings had been placed in storage bins by the construction crews. Though his mother wanted to sell some of those items to those in need, Andrew forbade her from doing it. He kept all of her items in his closet alongside the presents. One hot July day, Andrew went into his sister’s room, which remained intact through the fire, despite the amount of damage. He couldn’t help but cry. He takes a picture frame from off of her dresser and studies it sadly. It contained a photograph of them giving peace signs, while on a trip. He places it face down on the dresser and gets on his knees. He starts to pray. Midway through, he stopped and put his head down on Agatha’s bed. Tears started to well up again and he cried. All the while, his parents watched from the doorframe with frowns on their faces. In present day, Andrew sat with his head down on Agatha’s tombstone, tears rolling down his face. He kept repeating a single phrase in his sadness. “I’m sorry. I’m sorry. I’m sorry. I’m…” He sighs. Suddenly, a bright light caught his eye. He began to hear the sound of tracks being made in the snow. He turns around and comes eye to eye with Agatha. She had an angelic glow around her, and a faint outline of wings on her back. Andrew quickly turns back to face the tombstone. “Why are you here?” “You put all this pressure on yourself, yet...it wasn’t your fault. Won’t you look at me? I’m here to see you, brother.” “I don’t deserve to look upon you. Especially with the sins I have caused.” Agatha frowns. “Do you think I hate you for what you did? I know you just wanted to show how much you loved me…” “Who cares what the original intent was?! I committed a sin! As an angel, you should know that!” Andrew runs off, distraught and upset. Agatha stood with a sad expression. “Brother…you fool...” She vanishes into thin air. Andrew sat in his bedroom, not making a sound. His parents came into the room, full of concern. “Andrew…” “....what?” “Don’t you think it’s time to move on?” “No. How could you say that?” His mother looks to the ground. “She’s gone, Andrew. Nothing can change what happened.” Andrew seems to ignore her words. “Agatha came to me in the cemetery.” Their eyes go wide. “She was an angel?” “Of course she was…” His parents look at each other. “Maybe she’s trying to help you.” “Help me? What, by making me feel worse?” Andrew’s parents leave the room, convinced nothing could change his mind. He went to sleep, thinking about his sister, and the things they did. They had sinned, he knew that. On November 15th, the same year his sister died, Andrew had convinced her to do something many would consider shameful. She pushed back a lot, knowing the implications of what he was wanting. “It’s...it’s not right…” Andrew simply didn’t listen to her arguments. “We love each other, don’t we?” “But...this isn’t the same as that…” “What’s that supposed to mean?” “Well...don’t you think our love is good enough?” Andrew looks to the ground. “I just want to leave proof that I love you.” She frowns. “Are you telling the truth?” “Yes, yes. Of course I am!” Agatha looks to the side and sighs. “Alright. J-just this once. We can only do it this one time...” Andrew nods. He locks his bedroom door, and closes the curtains on the windows. Him and his sister sat on his bed. They locked eyes, and then they started to kiss each other passionately. Yes, what you are reading is true. To the outside world, it just seemed as if Andrew and Agatha were close siblings, but in reality, they were closer than that. However, they had only gone as far as kissing one another. Intimacy was something they hadn’t considered and they didn’t plan on involving themselves in, which is why Agatha was hesitant when Andrew finally decided to ask her about it. As they continued to kiss, they began to lay on top of each other. Andrew slowly unbuttoned Agatha’s shirt, and she avoided looking him in the eyes. He then removes her pants, and unzips his own. Her eyes go wide and she looks to the ground uncomfortably. Andrew notices her discomfort and smiles. “Don’t worry. It’ll be over soon.” She looks up at him, smiles wryly and then they kiss. Over the next few minutes, Andrew touched her inner body with gentle movements. Agatha said nothing; she only moaned and held him tightly. Suddenly, her expression changed to one that was full of shock. She began to close her eyes and tears rolled down her cheeks. “Andrew…something is building inside of me...I’m scared.” “It’s ok...it’s ok, sis...I’m here for you...” She smiles, and they hold each other as Andrew fills the place he was touching within Agatha. They kiss again, and then Andrew looks to the ground. “I’m sorry for forcing this on you…” Agatha glances at him and smiles sadly. “It’s okay...I know why you did it.” They hug tightly. “I love you, sis.” “I love you, too.” They sat there hugging in silence, with smiles on their faces. In the present, Andrew slept in the fetal position, tears rolling down his face.

III

It was December 12th, and Phoebe was having the time of her life. Currently, her and several other students were decorating the classroom. Under the watchful eye of their teacher, they stood on shaky ladders and put garland on the ceiling. Phoebe was certainly happy to be around so many people who supported her. When she first moved to Yarmouth, the greeting was lukewarm at best. Clearly she underestimated the people of this town. When it comes to the holidays, their true colors are always shown. But though everyone was enjoying themselves, Andrew was the only one who sat in the corner, not doing anything. Phoebe glances at him and frowns. She wanted to talk to him, but would he listen after what she did yesterday? She sadly returns to the celebrations. After a few minutes of merrymaking, their teacher brought out candles. Several students were confused. Phoebe picked one of the candles up and studied it. “Mrs. Thee...what are these for?” “Well, children, there are things about me that you don’t know. My husband of 20 years passed away in 1983, a few days after Christmas. I’ve provided these candles for you all to light in remembrance of someone who you’ve lost.” As soon as she said this, several students grabbed candles and matches. Mrs. Thee smiles. “Write the person’s name on it, and you’ll have something to remember them by during Christmas.” Though the merry tone of the celebrations had been interrupted by this fairly sad activity, many students were glad to participate. Phoebe grabbed a candle of her own and wrote her parents’ names on it. She smiles sadly and lights the wick. A warm glow emitted from the small flame. Suddenly, she has a realization, and she looks towards Andrew. He still refused to join in. Out of all the activities, he could have done this one, right? She grabs another candle and lights the flame. She slowly walks over to Andrew and holds it out to him. “Don’t you want to write your sister’s name?” He looks away in anguish. “No...get that away from me…” Phoebe didn’t back down, clearly not understanding his discomfort. “It’s just a memento.” She holds it towards him still, and he whimpers. “Stop...I don’t want to do it.” “What is it with you?” Andrew couldn’t hold it any longer. His pyrophobia was setting off alarm bells in every part of his brain. “Leave me alone! I can’t bear to see another fire!” He pushes her back, and she stumbles, the candle falling from her hands. Flames began to spread onto the decorations, and several frightened students huddled into a corner. The fire became larger and larger as smoke started to fill the room. Andrew’s eyes were wide, he was frozen in fear. No one dared to move; they were too scared to get the fire extinguisher. The memory of his sister’s death was coming back to Andrew, and he couldn’t watch anymore. He ran out into the flames, as students called for him to back up. “Andrew! What are you doing?!” He slides under the teacher’s desk and grabs the fire extinguisher. He goes to put out the fire, but the thing just didn’t work. Eventually, he got it to spray, but the flames were high now. He braces for impact, when suddenly a bright light reaches his eyes. Immediately, all of the flames dissipated. When the smoke cleared, the figure of Agatha came into view, and she was still in her angelic form. The other students and Mrs. Thee stood in shock and amazement at the sight of her. “She’s an angel…” Andrew dropped the fire extinguisher and closed his eyes. “Why won’t you leave me alone?” Agatha places a hand on his shoulder, and forces him to open his eyes. “I made a promise to God that I would help you, and I don’t intend to break it.” “I don’t care what you told God! I just want to be alone. Just let me be…” Agatha grabs him by the collar with an angry expression. Her angelic form turns dark and cold. “I MAY BE AN ANGEL, BUT THAT DOESN’T MEAN I CAN’T FORCE YOU TO LISTEN TO ME! NOW SHUT UP AND LISTEN!” Andrew’s face was full of fear. “I get it, I get it! Please don’t hurt me…” She drops him, and her angelic form returns. “You have 19 days. If you don’t bring back our spirit by that time, you’ll be nothing. A shell of a person. I didn’t want to have to do this, but...it’s the only way.” Andrew starts to feel light in his hands and he looks down to see that his left hand was now transparent. His sister starts to fade away. “Wait! Aggy, I...I can’t do it without you…” Agatha looks back at him sadly. “I’m dead, brother. Now’s the time for you to do things on your own.” She disappears into thin air, leaving Andrew to stand quietly. The other students were all looking at him, the feeling of awe still lingering in their faces. He walks over and picks up the knocked over candle. He places it on his desk, and lights it, watching the flame slowly move back and forth. He grabs a marker and writes Agatha’s name on the front. After a few minutes, he turned around and faced the students. “Well? Are we just going to leave the decorations on the ground?” Their faces all lit up, and Phoebe smiles, a tear falling down her cheek. Following this, the students remade all of their Christmas decorations, with help from Andrew. His skill at design made them all impressed, and Phoebe was starting to realize why he was considered the best at decorating in the past. It had been years since Andrew was this happy. In fact, the celebrating which was just happening in the classroom began to spread throughout the whole school. Word got out across the town that Andrew had finally got his spirit back. The school board was so impressed that they allowed him to decorate the entire building. Him and Phoebe were putting garland on the outside of the school one day, specifically December 16th, when he decided to finally talk to her again. “Hey, uh, Phobe?” “Hm?” “I wanted to say that...I’m sorry for what happened in the cemetery the other day…” She smiles. “It’s fine...I understand what you’re going through. I’m sorry as well. I know I said some horrible things, too.” Andrew stared at her for several minutes and then looked to the ground. “What? Something wrong?” “No, it’s nothing…” Actually, it wasn’t nothing at all. Andrew was beginning to feel odd in his chest. Right now, all he was thinking about was the fact that he never realized how attractive Phoebe was. He hadn’t noticed her beautiful light brown hair and freckles before this. He tried to push the thought out of his mind. He was supposed to be avoiding this sort of thing. His sister was the one for him, not some other girl he didn’t know. He returns to decorating. It was quiet for several minutes. Suddenly, Phoebe broke the silence as Andrew was putting lights on the garland. “Hey, Andrew...um...if you want, I could come over and...help you decorate your house…” Andrew’s eyes went wide and then he shook his head. “No...I’ll do it myself...I don’t need your help…” Phoebe glances at him and frowns. “I see…” They continued their work in silence once again, when all of sudden, the lights in Andrew’s hands fell straight through. He goes to pick them up, but his hands seem to be non-existent. Andrew starts to feel angry. “But I did everything you asked!” He runs off in anger as Phoebe stands with a worried expression. “Andrew...” He kept running and running, eventually ending up in the middle of a park seating area. He sits down on the water fountain edge and sighs. Suddenly, he felt an angelic presence and slammed his fist down. “Why am I still turning? You said I need to find the spirit! Isn’t that what I did?” Agatha places a glowing hand on his shoulder. “You still refuse to find someone else.” “Someone else- how can I do such a thing? I love you! I can’t just-” She covers his mouth. “You have someone new right in front of you. She shares many things with you, and yet…” Agatha sighs. She pulls out a piece of mistletoe and hands it to him. He finds that his right hand is full again and he takes the mistletoe. “I just don’t think I can let you go…” Agatha frowns. “I appreciate the fact that you still care about me, but...you have to move on. I’m sorry...that’s my final word.” She fades away once again. Andrew looks down at the mistletoe. “Alright. You win…” He walks silently out of the park and heads for Phoebe’s house. He knocks twice, and she answers after a few minutes. “Andrew?” He looks to the ground. “Can I talk with you?” Phoebe smiles. “Of course…” She grabs her jacket and comes outside. They sit down on her house’s front steps. “So...what did you want to talk about?” Andrew looks to the ground. “Are you really like me?” Phoebe stares at him for a few minutes and then nods silently. “It was 2 years ago. My parents were stationed in the military and I was in the care of a friend. Though time passed, they still hadn’t come back. Eventually I learned they died in an ambush. I had to spend Christmas alone, and I was forced to live with my uncle. He’s nothing like my parents. He’s vile and I wish I could just run away…” A tear rolls down her cheek.
